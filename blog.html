Thoughts on travel, life, and everything in between.

Hidden Charms of Bosnia

Four days in a country most travelers skip over, with reasons to regret that. Sarajevo sits at the intersection of Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian history, with visible scars from the 1990s still present. Covers the Stari Most bridge in Mostar, the wartime tunnel museum, the strange familiarity of a Muslim-majority Balkan country for a Nepali visitor, and the quiet of a place still figuring out what it wants to be.

Exploring India’s Spiritual Treasures

A solo trip across the Nepal border to trace the Buddhist circuit through Bodh Gaya, Varanasi, and Sarnath. Covers the 1am border crossing, the atmosphere under the Bodhi Tree, cremation ghats at dawn, and what it means to visit these places as someone who grew up nominally Buddhist but had never thought seriously about what that meant.

The US Experience

Six weeks in New York City doing AI research at NYU, with side trips through the American South. Covers the strangeness of New York compared to Kathmandu, the cognitive adjustment of being in the country most of the world orbits around, and the surprising warmth of the South, which was not the part of America I expected to like most.

My Journey in Debating

Four years in competitive debate, from a first tournament humiliation to representing Nepal nationally. Covers the early years of sneaking out of the school hostel to download debate videos on a slow connection, the culture of Nepali debate circuits, what the activity taught about argument and stubbornness, and why stopping was the right decision.
