Miscellaneous

Notes, transcripts, and other writing that doesn’t fit elsewhere.

The Information Bottleneck: Naomi on Understanding Training

A Kempner Fellow at Harvard and incoming BU professor on why training dynamics matter more than final weights. Covers grokking and phase transitions, what the loss curve hides, data quality, multilingual interlingua, sparse autoencoders and interpretability, language as the dominant modality, and why non-determinism across training runs is a deeper problem than the field acknowledges.

The Information Bottleneck: Stefano Ermon on Diffusion Language Models

Stanford professor and Inception AI CEO Stefano Ermon on why diffusion models are a better foundation for language generation than autoregressive transformers. Covers the history of score-based diffusion (2019), discrete diffusion theory, Mercury 2's inference speed advantage, hardware constraints, and what the architecture implies for AGI timelines.

Brainstorm Session: Generalizing Backprop

A lab discussion led by Yoshua Bengio on whether backpropagation can be generalized beyond smooth, differentiable computations. Covers the curse of backprop’s own success, the case against REINFORCE at scale, the memory problem for biological credit assignment, compartmentalization through abstract models, intermediate rewards and learned credit machinery, discrete actions and entropy, and the surprising smoothness of deep network loss surfaces.

World Modeling Workshop 2026: Day 1

Six talks at Mila on world models, from Schmidhuber’s 1990 work to LeCun’s JEPA to robots that learn from video. My notes from a day where everyone seemed to agree the field is at an interesting moment but disagree on everything else.

World Modeling Workshop 2026: Panel Discussion

LeCun, Bengio, Shirley Ho, Shuran Song, and Alessandro Lazaric at the World Modeling Workshop 2026, moderated by Randall Balestriero. Covers the input-space versus latent-space debate, hierarchical representations and where supervision for them comes from, error accumulation in neural simulators, causality and agency, and the relative merits of world models versus policies for generalization and sample efficiency.
