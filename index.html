I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at [University Name] in the [Lab Name] led by [Advisor Name]. I received my Ph.D. in 2023 from [University Name], where I was supervised by [Supervisor Name] and supported by an NSF GRFP.

I aim to develop AI agents that can intelligently communicate and collaborate with people and each other. My work addresses three key problems:

  1. Multi-agent communication and collaboration
    A central focus of my work, which has led to work on multi-LLM multi-round discussions/debates, distilling multi-agent behavior, pragmatic/verbalized uncertainty, and persuasion.
  2. Grounded agents through inputs and actions
    Another line of my work covers multimodal grounding and converting language to action through semantic parsing, text-to-code, and learning abstractions and skills.
  3. Handling uncertainty, ambiguity, and underspecification
    Developing safe and robust agents means handling uncertainty, ambiguity, and underspecification. As we scale up tasks, underspecification and ambiguity will become increasingly relevant, especially when predicting actions/grounding to the world. My work covers calibration and uncertainty especially in connection with implicit phenomena such as vagueness, underspecification, and ambiguity.
