Research

I am a senior at NYU Abu Dhabi studying Mathematics and Computer Science. I work with Professor Keith Ross on energy-based world models.

Most of my past work is in LLM reasoning and interpretability. The question I keep coming back to is how to build systems that actually understand the world rather than predict the next token. Right now that means world models, model-based RL, and taking neuroscience more seriously than most ML researchers tend to.

I tend to learn by getting the big picture first and then filling in the details. This works well in research and badly in exams.

Outside Work

I swim and play tennis at NYUAD. I have been to over 25 countries, mostly because staying in one place makes me restless. I describe myself as competitive, which is a polite way of saying I care too much about things that do not always matter.

I find the intersection of biology and intelligence genuinely fascinating, less as a metaphor and more as a research direction. Most of the interesting questions about AI, I think, are also questions about how brains work.
